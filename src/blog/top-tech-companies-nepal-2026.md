## FAQ: Tech Companies in Nepal

### What are the top tech companies in Nepal?

The largest tech companies in Nepal by employee count include Deerwalk Services, Leapfrog Technology, Cotiviti Nepal, Verisk Nepal, and CloudFactory. These companies each employ 400+ people in Nepal, primarily serving international clients in healthcare, insurance, and AI data services.

### Which AI companies are based in Nepal?

Nepal has a growing AI sector with companies like Zunkiree Labs (AI search and RAG), Fusemachines (AI education and consulting), CloudFactory (AI training data), Cotiviti Nepal (healthcare AI), and Docsumo (document AI). These companies work on machine learning, natural language processing, computer vision, and data labeling.

### What is the average salary for software developers in Nepal?

Software developer salaries in Nepal vary widely. Junior developers typically earn NPR 400,000-800,000 annually, mid-level developers earn NPR 800,000-1,500,000, and senior developers can earn NPR 1,500,000-3,000,000+. AI/ML specialists and those with international experience command higher salaries.

### How do I find software development jobs in Nepal?

Major job platforms include LinkedIn, MeroJob, JobsNepal, and Kumarijob. Most top tech companies actively recruit through their websites. Tech meetups, Slack communities like NepalJS, and university career fairs are also common channels.

### Are there good tech startups in Nepal?

Yes, Nepal has a growing startup ecosystem. Notable startups include Khalti (fintech), Foodmandu (food delivery), Tootle (ride-hailing), Hamro Patro (content platform), and Daraz (e-commerce). The ecosystem is supported by incubators like Entrepreneurs for Nepal (E4N) and increasing seed funding availability.

### What industries do Nepali tech companies serve?

Nepali tech companies primarily serve:
- **Healthcare**: Deerwalk, Cotiviti, Verisk
- **Fintech/Banking**: F1Soft, eSewa, Khalti
- **AI/Data**: Fusemachines, CloudFactory, Zunkiree Labs
- **E-commerce**: Daraz, Sastodeal, Thulo
- **Enterprise Software**: Leapfrog, Javra, Proshore

### How does Nepal compare to India for software outsourcing?

Nepal offers 20-40% lower costs than India with comparable quality for many projects. English proficiency is high, and time zone alignment with Europe and parts of Asia is favorable. However, India has more scale, more specialized talent pools, and more developed infrastructure. Nepal works well for teams of 10-50 people where personal relationships matter.

## Conclusion

Nepal's tech industry in 2026 spans AI research labs, software powerhouses, and ambitious startups. While challenges remain—infrastructure, talent retention, and access to capital—the trajectory is clearly upward.

For organizations seeking technology partners, Nepal offers compelling value: quality engineering at competitive rates, strong English communication, and a growing pool of specialized talent in AI, healthcare tech, and fintech.

The companies profiled here represent the leading edge of what's possible from Kathmandu and beyond. Whether you need an AI implementation partner, software development team, or are simply curious about emerging tech hubs, Nepal deserves attention.
